What you are looking at: A topic is a recurring theme automatically learned from the queries (e.g. "Financial Fraud"). A cluster is a flat grouping of similar queries — adjust the Granularity slider to get more or fewer groups. An annotation unit is an adaptively-sized group chosen as the right scope for writing one rubric: not too broad (the root contains all queries) and not too specific (individual leaves are single queries). Theme diversity = how mixed the topics are (high -> varied queries -> richer rubric needed). Split evenness = how equal the two sub-groups were when this unit was formed. Priority combines diversity (50%), group size (30%), and split evenness (20%).
